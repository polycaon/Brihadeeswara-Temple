Thanjavur's Architectural Marvel

Built by Raja Raja Chola I in the 11th century

Standing tall at the center of Thanjavur, this magnificent structure represents the pinnacle of Chola dynasty's architectural brilliance. The temple's vimana rises to 216 feet, making it one of the tallest of its kind in the world.

Built entirely of granite, the temple showcases the engineering mastery of ancient Tamil architects. The massive stone blocks were transported from quarries over 60 kilometers away using just manpower and ingenuity.

The temple complex, spread over 40 acres, features intricate sculptures, stunning frescoes, and the famous 80-ton capstone at its summit - a testament to the remarkable achievements of 11th-century craftsmanship.
